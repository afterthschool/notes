# Control and Coordination in Living Organisms

In the previous chapter, we looked at life processes involved in the maintenance functions in living organisms. There, we had started with a notion we all have — that if we see something moving, it is alive.

Some of these movements are, in fact, the result of growth, as seen in plants. A seed germinates and grows, and we can observe that the seedling moves over the course of a few days. It pushes soil aside and emerges. However, if its growth were to stop, these movements would not occur.

On the other hand, some movements, as observed in many animals and some plants, are not connected with growth. A cat running, children playing on swings, and buffaloes chewing cud are examples of movements that are not caused by growth.

## Movement and Life

Why do we associate such visible movements with life?

A possible answer is that we think of movement as a response to a change in the environment of the organism. For example:
- A cat may run because it has seen a mouse.
- Plants grow towards sunlight.
- Children swing to experience pleasure and fun.
- Buffaloes chew cud to break down tough food for better digestion.

We also respond to environmental changes:
- When bright light is focused on our eyes, we react.
- When we touch a hot object, we quickly withdraw our hand.

These responses involve movement that helps protect or benefit the organism.

## Controlled Responses

If we think more deeply, we realize that all such movements in response to the environment are carefully controlled.

Each type of environmental change triggers an appropriate response. For example:
- When talking to friends in class, we whisper instead of shouting.

This shows that the type of movement depends on the stimulus (triggering event).

## Control and Coordination

Such controlled movement must be linked to:
1. Recognition of environmental changes
2. Selection of the correct response

In other words, living organisms require systems that provide **control and coordination**.

In multicellular organisms, this is achieved through specialized tissues that handle these functions efficiently.
