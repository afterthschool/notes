

# 7.3 Sexual Reproduction

## Introduction

Sexual reproduction involves the participation of **two individuals**.

- Both **male and female** are required
- Examples:
  - Bulls and cows → calves
  - Hens and cocks → chicks

---

## Key Question

- What is the significance of sexual reproduction?
- What are the limitations of asexual reproduction?

---

# 7.3.1 Why the Sexual Mode of Reproduction?

## Role of DNA Copying

- Reproduction involves:
  - **Copying of DNA**
  - **Copying of cellular structures**

- DNA copying:
  - Is **not perfectly accurate**
  - Leads to **variations**

---

## Importance of Variation

- Variations occur in populations
- Not all individuals benefit, but:
  - Some variations help in **survival**

---

## Limitation of Asexual Reproduction

- Variations arise **slowly**
- DNA copying is:
  - Accurate enough to prevent major errors
  - But slow in generating diversity

---

## Need for Faster Variation

To increase variation:
- Combine DNA from **different individuals**
- Each individual already has accumulated variations

### Result:
- New combinations of variations
- Greater diversity in offspring

---

## Role of Sexual Reproduction

Sexual reproduction:
- Combines DNA from **two individuals**
- Produces **new genetic combinations**

---

## Problem of DNA Doubling

If DNA from two individuals combines:
- DNA content would **double each generation**
- This would disrupt cellular control

---

## Solution: Meiosis

To solve this problem:

- Special cells called **germ cells** are formed
- These cells contain:
  - **Half the number of chromosomes**
  - **Half the DNA content**

### Process:
- Formation of germ cells occurs through **meiosis**

---

## Formation of Zygote

- Two germ cells (gametes) combine
- This restores:
  - Normal chromosome number
  - Normal DNA content

---

## Gamete Specialization

As organisms become more complex:

- Germ cells become **specialized**

### Types of Gametes:

#### 1. Male Gamete
- Smaller
- Usually **motile** (can move)

#### 2. Female Gamete
- Larger
- Contains **stored food (energy)**

---

## Importance of Food Storage

- The **zygote** needs energy to:
  - Grow
  - Develop into a complex organism

---

## Outcome

- Sexual reproduction leads to:
  - Genetic variation
  - Specialized reproductive structures
  - Differences between male and female organisms

  # 7.3.2 Sexual Reproduction in Flowering Plants

## Reproductive Parts of a Flower

In **angiosperms (flowering plants)**, reproduction takes place in the **flower**.

### Main Parts of a Flower:
- Sepals  
- Petals  
- Stamens  
- Pistil  

---

## Functions of Flower Parts

- **Stamens and Pistil** → Reproductive parts (contain germ cells)  
- **Petals and Sepals** → Help in:
  - Protection of the flower (sepals)
  - Attraction of pollinators like insects (petals)

---

## Types of Flowers

### 1. Unisexual Flowers
- Contain **either stamens or pistil**
- Examples:
  - Papaya  
  - Watermelon  

---

### 2. Bisexual Flowers
- Contain **both stamens and pistil**
- Examples:
  - Hibiscus  
  - Mustard  

---

## Male Reproductive Part: Stamen

- Produces **pollen grains**
- Pollen grains:
  - Are **yellowish in colour**
  - Can be seen as powder
  - Contain male germ cells

---

## Female Reproductive Part: Pistil

- Located at the **centre of the flower**
- It is the female reproductive organ

### Pistil consists of three parts:
- Stigma  
- Style  
- Ovary

## Structure of Pistil

The pistil consists of three parts:

- **Ovary** → Swollen bottom part  
- **Style** → Middle elongated part  
- **Stigma** → Terminal sticky part  

---

## Ovules and Gametes

- The **ovary contains ovules**
- Each ovule contains an **egg cell (female gamete)**

---

## Fertilisation

- The **male germ-cell** from the pollen grain fuses with the **female gamete** in the ovule  
- This fusion is called **fertilisation**

### Result:
- Formation of a **zygote**
- The zygote develops into a **new plant**

---

## Pollination

For fertilisation to occur, pollen must be transferred from **stamen to stigma**.

### Types of Pollination:

#### 1. Self-Pollination
- Pollen is transferred within the **same flower**

#### 2. Cross-Pollination
- Pollen is transferred from **one flower to another**

### Agents of Pollination:
- Wind  
- Water  
- Animals  

---

## Pollen Tube Formation

- After landing on the stigma:
  - A **tube grows out of the pollen grain**
  - It travels through the **style**
  - Reaches the **ovary**

---

## Formation of Seed and Fruit

After fertilisation:

1. **Zygote divides** → forms an **embryo**
2. **Ovule develops** → becomes a **seed**
3. Seed forms a **tough protective coat**
4. **Ovary develops** → becomes a **fruit**
5. Other parts (petals, sepals, stamens, style, stigma):
   - **Dry up and fall off**

---

## Germination

- The **seed contains the embryo (future plant)**
- Under suitable conditions:
  - The seed develops into a **seedling**

> This process is called **germination**

# 7.3.3 Reproduction in Human Beings

## Introduction

Humans reproduce through the **sexual mode of reproduction**.

---

## Growth and Development

As humans grow:
- Height increases
- Teeth develop (milk teeth are replaced by permanent teeth)

These changes are part of **general body growth**.

---

## Changes During Adolescence

During early teenage years, additional changes occur that are not just related to growth.

### General Changes (in both boys and girls):

- Growth of hair in:
  - Armpits
  - Genital area
- Hair may also appear on:
  - Arms
  - Legs
  - Face
- Skin becomes **oily**
- Development of **pimples**
- Increased awareness of body and others

---

## Changes Specific to Girls

- Increase in **breast size**
- Darkening of the **nipples**
- Beginning of **menstruation**

---

## Changes Specific to Boys

- Growth of **facial hair**
- Voice begins to **crack**
- Occasional enlargement and erection of the **penis**

---

## Nature of These Changes

- Occur gradually over **months or years**
- Do not happen at the same time for everyone
- Show variation among individuals

These changes are part of **sexual maturation**.

---

## Puberty

- The stage when reproductive tissues mature is called **puberty**
- Occurs during adolescence

### Reason:
- Early growth focuses on increasing body size
- As growth slows, **reproductive organs begin to mature**

---

## Role of Sexual Maturation

- Sexual reproduction requires **fusion of germ cells from two individuals**
- Maturity must be **recognisable to others**

### Indicators:
- Hair growth patterns
- Body changes

---

## Reproduction Mechanism in Humans

In animals like humans:
- Germ cells are transferred through **mating**
- This involves:
  - Internal transfer of germ cells
  - Fusion inside the body

---

## Specialised Reproductive Organs

- Organs are required for:
  - Transfer of germ cells
  - Development of offspring

### In Males:
- Penis (capable of erection for transfer)

### In Females:
- Reproductive organs for:
  - Carrying the baby
  - Supporting development

---

## Development of Offspring

- In mammals:
  - Baby develops inside the **mother’s body**
  - After birth, the baby is **breast-fed**

---

## Conclusion

- Human reproduction involves:
  - Sexual maturation
  - Development of specialised organs
  - Internal fertilisation and development

# 7.3.3 (a) Male Reproductive System

## Overview

The male reproductive system consists of:
- Organs that **produce germ cells (sperms)**
- Organs that **transport sperms to the site of fertilisation**

---

## Testes

- Site of **sperm production**
- Located outside the abdominal cavity in the **scrotum**

### Reason:
- Sperm formation requires a **lower temperature** than normal body temperature

### Additional Function:
- Secretes the hormone **testosterone**

#### Role of Testosterone:
- Regulates **sperm formation**
- Causes changes during **puberty** in boys

---

## Transport of Sperms

- Sperms travel through the **vas deferens**
- The vas deferens joins a tube from the **urinary bladder**

### Urethra:
- Acts as a **common passage** for:
  - Sperms
  - Urine

---

## Accessory Glands

Along the vas deferens, certain glands add secretions:

### Glands Involved:
- **Prostate gland**
- **Seminal vesicles**

### Functions:
- Add fluid to sperms
- Provide **nutrition**
- Help in **easy transport**

---

## Structure of Sperms

- Very **small cells**
- Consist of:
  - **Genetic material (head)**
  - **Long tail**

### Function of Tail:
- Helps sperm **move towards the female gamete**

# 7.3.3 (b) Female Reproductive System

## Overview

The female reproductive system consists of organs that:
- **Produce eggs (female germ cells)**
- Support **fertilisation and development of the embryo**

---

## Ovaries

- Produce **eggs (ova)**
- Also produce **hormones**

### At Birth:
- Ovaries contain **thousands of immature eggs**

### After Puberty:
- Some eggs begin to **mature**
- Typically, **one egg is released every month**

---

## Oviduct (Fallopian Tube)

- Carries the egg from the ovary to the uterus
- Site where **fertilisation may occur**

---

## Uterus

- A **bag-like elastic structure**
- Formed by the joining of the two oviducts

### Function:
- Site for **development of the embryo**
- Lining becomes:
  - **Thick**
  - Rich in **blood supply**
- Prepares every month to receive the embryo

---

## Cervix and Vagina

- **Cervix**:
  - Narrow opening of the uterus

- **Vagina**:
  - Passage through which:
    - Sperms enter during intercourse
    - Baby is delivered at birth

---

## Fertilisation Process

1. Sperms enter through the **vagina**
2. Travel upward to the **oviduct**
3. May fuse with the egg → **fertilisation**
4. Forms a **zygote**

---

## Embryo Development

- Zygote divides repeatedly → forms **embryo**
- Embryo implants in the **uterine lining**
- Develops into a **foetus**

---

## Placenta

- Special tissue connecting mother and embryo

### Structure:
- Disc embedded in uterine wall
- Contains **villi (finger-like projections)**

### Functions:
- Transfers:
  - **Nutrients (glucose)**
  - **Oxygen**
- Removes:
  - **Waste products** from embryo

---

## Duration of Development

- Development inside the uterus lasts about **9 months**

---

## Birth Process

- Child is born due to:
  - **Rhythmic contractions** of uterine muscles 

# 7.3.3 (c) What Happens When the Egg is Not Fertilised?

- If the egg is not fertilised:
  - It survives for about **one day**

- The uterus prepares every month for possible pregnancy:
  - Its lining becomes **thick and spongy**
  - Meant to **nourish the embryo**

- If fertilisation does not occur:
  - The uterine lining is **no longer needed**
  - It breaks down and is removed from the body

### Menstruation

- The shedding of the uterine lining:
  - Comes out through the **vagina**
  - Contains **blood and mucus**

- This cycle:
  - Occurs roughly **every month**
  - Lasts about **2 to 8 days**

---

# 7.3.3 (d) Reproductive Health

## Readiness for Reproduction

- Sexual maturation is a **gradual process**
- Physical maturity does not always mean:
  - Emotional readiness
  - Mental readiness

### Challenges in Decision-Making

- Pressure from:
  - Friends
  - Family
  - Society
  - Government policies

- These pressures can make decisions about reproduction difficult

---

## Health Risks

- Sexual activity can lead to transmission of diseases

### Sexually Transmitted Diseases (STDs):

- **Bacterial:**
  - Gonorrhoea
  - Syphilis  

- **Viral:**
  - Warts  
  - HIV-AIDS  

---

## Prevention of STDs

- Use of **condoms**:
  - Acts as a protective covering
  - Reduces risk of disease transmission

---

## Pregnancy and Its Impact

- Sexual activity can lead to **pregnancy**
- Pregnancy requires:
  - Physical readiness
  - Mental preparedness

- If unprepared:
  - Can negatively affect health

---

## Contraceptive Methods

Methods to prevent pregnancy:

### 1. Mechanical Barriers
- Prevent sperm from reaching the egg
- Examples:
  - Condoms
  - Vaginal coverings

---

### 2. Hormonal Methods
- Prevent egg release
- Taken as:
  - Oral pills
- May have **side effects**

---

### 3. Intrauterine Devices (IUDs)
- Examples:
  - Loop
  - Copper-T
- Placed in uterus
- May cause irritation or side effects

---

### 4. Surgical Methods

- **Vasectomy (male):**
  - Blocks vas deferens

- **Tubectomy (female):**
  - Blocks fallopian tubes

- Prevents fertilisation

- Risks:
  - Infection if not performed properly

---

## Misuse of Medical Methods

- Surgical abortion can be misused:
  - Example: **Sex-selective abortion**

- This leads to:
  - Decline in **female population**

- Prenatal sex determination is:
  - **Legally prohibited**

---

## Population and Society

- Population size depends on:
  - Birth rate
  - Death rate

- Rapid population growth:
  - Can affect **standard of living**

- However:
  - Inequality may also be a major cause of poor living standards
